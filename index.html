What drives each side

Derived from real customer discovery interviews — here's what we learned about each group's motivations.

🧭 Founders
Job to be done

Identify a validated problem worth solving

Before investing months of effort, founders need structured evidence that real, recurring pain exists — and that people would pay for relief.

Pain point

Finding real users to interview is the hardest step

Cold outreach gets ignored. Friends give biased feedback. Locating honest, willing strangers who match your target user profile is a significant barrier at the earliest stage.

Desired gain

Evidence-based confidence to commit

After five structured conversations with real problem facers, founders gain the conviction to build — or the clarity to pivot before wasting resources.

🗣️ Problem Facers
Job to be done

Get a real solution to a recurring frustration

They deal with the same problem week after week, settling for inefficient workarounds because the market hasn't delivered what they need.

Pain point

The feedback loop to builders is broken

They post on social media, leave app reviews, tell friends — but the people with the skills and motivation to build a real fix never hear their voice.

Desired gain

Influence what gets built next

When a committed founder says "I want to deeply understand your problem," it's validating. Problem facers want to co-create solutions and be first in line when they launch.

How it works

This isn't another feedback forum.

Most platforms give you noise. problemfirst gives you structured, critical insight from people with real context.

🚫 Typical: random opinions, shallow likes, zero structure
✅ Here: structured critique, logical analysis, direct conversations
1
📝 Structured Input
📋Problems and ideas are posted in a structured format

Every submission follows a guided framework — not open-ended text boxes. This ensures founders and critics have enough context to respond with substance.

🎯Problem posts capture who's affected, frequency, pain level (1–5), current workaround, and willingness to pay
💡Idea posts require target users, the problem being solved, and key assumptions — forcing clarity before feedback begins
2
🔬 Structured Critique
🧠Feedback is categorized, critical, and actionable

Responses are categorized by type — logical flaw, assumption challenge, improvement suggestion, or real-world experience. This separates signal from noise and rewards depth over volume.

🔍Logical critique — responders identify gaps in reasoning and untested assumptions, not just react with opinions
🏆Best Insight recognition — founders mark the most valuable feedback. Optional bounties incentivize quality over quantity
3
🤝 Direct Conversation
☕Founders connect directly with real users

Public critique is a starting point. The real value comes from direct, private exchanges between a founder and someone who lives the problem every day.

💬Follow-up questions and messaging — go deeper than any public thread can reach, with focused back-and-forth
📅 15-Minute Customer Discovery Interviews

Founders can request a short, structured call with a problem facer. No cold outreach, no favors — just a willing participant and a focused agenda. Based on our interviews, this is the single highest-value interaction for early-stage validation.
